Realizing The American Dream?

a tale of the H-1B visa

Survey shows more than 25% of Americans think the "American Dream" is failing.

Yet, every year, millions of working class people all over the world aspire to this seemingly unattainable fantasy,hoping one day to live the ideal life of comfort and happiness that the American Dream promises.










For foreign nationals, that dream is often made possible with a thing called H-1B.

Although the U.S. government does not have adequate data for the total number of foreign employmers working in America, it is estimated that there are roughly 1.42 million temporary foreign workers who are authorized to be employed in the U.S. labor market. That number is approximately equal to 1% of the U.S. labor force. Perhaps the H-1B visa does not matter much to 99% people in the labor force, but to the other 1% who need some kind of work visa as work authorization, H-1B visa is the most popular path to do so, accounting for more than 32% of total work visa issued in the United States.

But getting a work visa in the first place is never easy. Since the H-1B was created in 1990s, Congress has limited the number of H-1Bs made available each year. As of now, the current annual numerical limit "cap" is 65,000 visas, with an addition of 20,000 H-1B visas for foreign professionals who graduate with a master’s degree or doctorate from a U.S. institution of higher learning.

But over the years, USCIS has received more petitions than there are visa numbers available as there are always a greater number of skilled workers applying for the H-1B visas.

So what happen when the number of applications exceeds the yearly quota? USCIS would conduct a lottery to determine which employers’ petitions for H-1B workers will be processed. Yes, pure luck.

However, even if the applicant get drawn, it does not guarantee for approval by the USCIS. There is a chance that the USCIS will issue A Request For Evidence (RFE) to the applicants to provie additional supporting materials for their applications, and in the worst case, a rejection would be issued.

Okay, but let's not forget, to qualify for the H-1B application, first the worker needs an employer sponsorship, which is not easy to get for some young professions or people working in certain industries.

Every year, all new petitions make it through the lottery will either get approve or get denied. The graph on the left show the total number of initial approvals andd the total number of initial denails for first-time H-1B applicants from the year of 2010 to 2019.

Once approved, the H-1B visa holder is valid to work for up to three years, and they could renew for an additional three years. USCIS also requires H-1B holders to report and update their H-1B visa if they switch company. There is no need to draw a lottery in the above scenarios, but the USCIS reserve the rights to decide that the H-1B holder is not longer eligible and withdraw their work statuss.

On April 18, 2017, Pressidednt Trump signed the Buy American and Hire American Executive Order, which, just by the title itself, appears to be an anti H-1B act.

The number of total approval under Trump's addministration drop much more drastically than the number of initial approval, suggesting that USCIS has implemented a much more rigorous check on the H-1B visa holders to eliminiate fraud and abuse.
